Interface Analysis: Modeling Cultural Theory Through Digital Interfaces

Spectral Negotiation Interface

This interface serves as a foundational apparatus for theoretical reflection, operating at the intersection of quantum entanglement and multistability. It functions as a meditation interface that facilitates switching between rational and symbolic modes of thinking.

Key aspects:
  • Enables awareness of present/absent elements in theoretical thinking
  • Most abstract yet most useful for theoretical reflection
  • Functions as a cognitive switching mechanism

Information Architecture Navigator

An interactive appendix focused on modeling Cook's death through a complex system of entities, sub-entities, morphisms, and actions. Based on Marshall Sahlins' "How 'Natives' Think: About Captain Cook, For Example," this interface represents an ambitious attempt to systematize historical narrative.

Key aspects:
  • Complex modeling of historical events using notebook LM
  • Serves as conceptual foundation for simulation work
  • Staging ground for flux-field development

View Interface →

Reflective Questions

  1. How does the process of breaking down historical events into entities and morphisms both clarify and potentially oversimplify complex cultural interactions?
  2. What assumptions about causality and agency are embedded in the way I've modeled relationships between entities?
  3. How does the computational representation of Sahlins' work reflect or distort his original arguments?
  4. What cultural nuances or contextual elements were lost in the process of formal modeling?
  5. How has the "monster" nature of this interface revealed the challenges of representing cultural complexity?
  6. What does the difficulty in "wrangling" this model tell us about the nature of historical interpretation?

Flux Field Visualization

A PIE-BDI Network ForceGraph implementation exploring theoretical form generation. This interface visualizes the entanglements between Sahlins' and Obeyesekere's theoretical frameworks through an interactive network visualization.

Key aspects:
  • Visualizes theoretical interference patterns
  • Creates intuitive language for theoretical relationships
  • Demonstrates theoretical entanglements through force-directed layout

Narrative Log Interface

A simplified mechanical approach to cultural modeling using hostile architecture as a framework. This interface demonstrates the evolution of the project's assumptions and methodology through narrative generation.

Key aspects:
  • Simplified mechanics based on node distances
  • Generates narrative logs for theoretical debugging
  • Functions as training exercise for flux-field refactoring
